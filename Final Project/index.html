Film Developing, Processing, Scanning & More

Welcome to blk.film lab, your destination for professional film development and scanning services. We are based in Montgomery, Alabama, but are proud to process film from all around the world. As a dedicated film lab, we specialize in processing both color and black-and-white film using state-of-the-art dip and dunk processors. Photographers trust us with their 35mm, medium format, and large format films, knowing we deliver the best quality, high-resolution digital files of their scans.

At blk.film lab, we pride ourselves on being a people's lab, fostering a diverse community of photographers who trust us with their creative visions. Whether you're a seasoned professional, a passionate hobbyist, or a newcomer to film photography, we are committed to providing a stellar experience. We offer personalized service and a collaborative approach to editing that ensures every client receives scans they love.

In addition to our exceptional film processing and scanning services, we host Film Drop. What's that, you ask? We purchase film at wholesale prices and pass those savings along to you, bundled with a lab credit! Our slogan, "Long Live Film," reflects our passion for preserving the art and craft of analog photography! We believe in the art of film and work tirelessly to keep it alive.

ALL FILM TYPES

We are a film lab that specializes in developing and scanning film.

We offer services for color, black and white, and slide film as well as prints for 35mm and medium format film.

COLOR - 35MM & 120MM FILM

Color Film Development, Scanning, and Prints.

35mm film is printed on 4x6 luster paper.

Medium formats (6x4.5, 6x6, and 6x7) is printed on luster paper without any cropping.

MONOCHROME - 35MM & 120MM FILM

Black and White Film Development, Scanning, and Prints.

35mm film is printed on 4x6 luster paper with no color cast.

Medium formats (6x4.5, 6x6, and 6x7) is printed on luster paper without any cropping.

E6 SLIDE FILM

E-6 Slide Film Development, Scanning, and Prints.

35mm film is printed on 4x6 luster paper.

Medium formats (6x4.5, 6x6, and 6x7) is printed on luster paper without any cropping.
